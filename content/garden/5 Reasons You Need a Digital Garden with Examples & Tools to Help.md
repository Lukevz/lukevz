# 5 Reasons You Need a Digital Garden with Examples & Tools to Help
#output/video 

## Intro
- I’ve been making videos for 14+ years – my early stuff was cringe, but it was *free*. No SEO, no “content strategy,” just making things because I wanted to.
- Over time, social media shifted from **self-expression → self-promotion**. Everything had to have a hook, a niche, a growth plan.
- As a multi-interest creative, that shift slowly made the internet feel **exhausting instead of energizing**. I was spending more time performing than exploring.
- I started craving a place online that felt like my old creative self again: messy, playful, low stakes.
- That’s when I discovered **digital gardens** – and they quietly changed how I think, create, and track my ideas.
---
## What is a Digital Garden?
* A traditional **blog** = polished, finished pieces. You research, edit, perfect… and once you hit publish, it’s “done.”
* A **digital garden** = the opposite:
  * You publish **drafts, notes, half-formed thoughts**.
  * Everything is **editable, revisable, deletable** – forever.
  * osts are less like articles and more like **living notes** you can update as you learn.
* It’s not about lowering quality; it’s about **lowering the pressure** so ideas can grow in public.
- Over time, your garden becomes a **map of your thinking**: you can see which ideas stayed, which changed, and which ones are truly evergreen.
---
## 5 Reasons You Need a Digital Garden
1. **It gives your messy ideas a home**
   - A place for drafts, half-thoughts, and unfinished notes
   - Lets you share work-in-progress without feeling embarrassed
   - Functions more like a playground for your brain than a polished portfolio

2. **It lets you publish like a human, not an algorithm**
   - No pressure to optimise for reach, timing, or trending topics
   - You share what you’re genuinely curious about in the moment
   - Lowers the performance pressure so making things feels fun again

3. **Your thinking becomes a living map, not a final take**
   - There’s no “final version” – notes can evolve as you learn
   - You can revisit, rewrite, and expand ideas over months or years
   - People don’t just see what you think, they see how your thinking changes

4. **You actually own your ideas (files over feeds)**
   - Often self-hosted or built on tools that keep your notes in your control
   - Your work isn’t trapped in a single app or platform that might disappear
   - Easier to back up, move, and reshape your ideas over time

5. Quantity quietly turns into quality 
   - The more low-pressure notes you publish, the more creative reps you get
   - Rough scraps slowly evolve into strong essays, videos, or projects
   - “Quietly” because there’s no algorithm or social pressure driving you – just your own curiosity

## 3 Styles of Digital Gardeners
- There are lots of examples that don’t fit in these boxes and many that overlap across all styles
- Where you choose to home your garden is up to you, but pick somewhere that will feel freeing and remove any unnecessary pressure
- A lot of digital gardening is writing, but it could be any form of expression

### The Writer
A digital studio for drafts, half-ideas, and future essays/scripts to live in public while they’re still messy.

**Good for:** bloggers, YouTubers, newsletter writers, and educators who are constantly developing ideas and want a low-pressure place to park outlines, brain dumps, and half-finished pieces that might later turn into “real” content.

**Examples**
https://www.floguo.com
https://joelhooks.com
https://salman.io/notes/
https://www.paulgraham.com/index.html
https://stephango.com/

### The Collector
A curated archive of the best stuff you consume: quotes, links, screenshots, highlights, and notes.

**Good for:** researchers, designers, product folks, and generally curious people who read/watch/listen a lot and don’t want their insights to disappear into bookmarks or random docs, but instead build into a personal “internet inside your head.”

**Examples**
https://maggieappleton.com/garden
https://www.iamrob.in/garden/
https://anthonyamar.fr/Welcome+in+my+mind+🧠
https://nikiv.dev
https://www.ssp.sh/brain/

### The Architect
A fully custom “personal OS” where your notes, projects, experiments, and even little apps or games all live inside a world you’ve designed.

**Good for:** product designers, developers, and tinkerers who enjoy building systems, interfaces, and playful experiences, and want their thinking space to double as a portfolio, sandbox, and art project.

**Examples**
ryOS – https://ryo.lu/
MitchIvin XP – https://mitchivin.com/
