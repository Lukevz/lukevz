# 7 habits / routines that regulate and bring me peace in 2026
#output/video 

[How I Stopped Burnout and Regulated My Nervous System | 7 Habits I Cultivated for 2026](https://youtu.be/1w45ID4tRBM)

One of my focuses for 2026 is to build a calmer mind and a regulated environment. I want to do less doomscrolling and more “bloomscrolling.” Less time jumping from one activity to the next or multitasking and more time staying in the moment. One device at a time, one habit at a time.

So here are 7 habits and routines that I have been cultivating that bring me peace.

## 1. Morning Pages
I’ve had a Ross and Rachel relationship with journaling for years, but recently I had a breakthrough. I was always trying to make my journaling future-proof, aesthetic, or serve some greater purpose. While some entries are definitely worth looking back on or prompt deeper insights, I found that what I really needed was a place to dump my thoughts.

This may seem obvious to a lot of people, but as a recovering people pleaser and perfectionist, this was – and still is – a difficult headspace to stay in. So my goal every morning of each weekday is to jot down all my thoughts, feelings, and ideas *before* starting work.

In 2025, I would basically wake up, make coffee, and immediatley hop on Slack or jump into work. Over the holiday break, I realized that what I really craved was having a slow start to my morning and feeling more in control. While I can’t sleep in as late as I want, doing something for me in a zero-pressure environment is exactly what I needed.

## 2. Moving Every Day
Working from home is an absolute blessing and I am very grateful for having the freedom and flexibility that I do. One thing I struggle with though is getting enough movement throughout the day. With back to back meetings or deep work sessions, it’s easy to get stuck in a groove.

For 2026, I’m trying to walk an average 7,500 steps each day, in addition to lifting 3x a week at the gym. I had the workout part down already, but actually just moving enough to stretch and get fresh air is something I’m trying to make a consistent habit. Moving a little bit every hour or after each meeting, refilling water,  making a coffee are also small ways that help me stretch and break the “grind”.

## 3. Scrapbooking
I’ve noticed a trend lately of people going more analog. Switching back to old iPods, using film cameras, and scrapbooking. Recently, my mom pulled out a scrapbook from when she was in high school and it was such an authentic, rare experience to go through those photos, notes, and keepsakes she had. She even had an accordion of the 1996 Atlanta Olympic games *with* their original tickets in absolute mint condition.

Those are the memory-tending experiences I’m after. So my wife and I recently bought a scrapbook, printed out some of our travel photos, and started collaging. It was such a simple, slow experience it really showed me how fast-paced every activity is these days. We spent almost 3 hours just sitting at our kitchen table looking over old photos and playing around with stickers. It made me feel like a kid again.

I like this activity because as a digital artist, it’s refreshing to have to commit to a design and not be able to “undo.” Plus, who doesn’t like stickers.

## 4. Cleaning As You Go
I like to consider myself a tidy person but I still end up with doompiles or missing cleaning days, and when the mess piles up I get more stressed out. Working from home is a privledge that should allow me to keep my house organized and clean, so what’s stopping me?

In tandem with always rushing on to the next task, I realized that I could easily afford a few extra minutes to clean up right after making a mess, as opposed to letting everything build up. This can look like:
- changing outfits and folding away your old clothes immediately
- putting away dirty dishes in the dishwasher
- replacing gear or equipment (camera, headphones, laptop)

It’s a bit extra work on the go, but I found myself a lot less stressed when the weekends rolled around knowing that I didn’t have hours of cleaning and catchup to do.

## 5. Photo Walks
I’ve been rekindling my love for photography and videography recently and one of the best ways to practice is to literally just get out and shoot. There’s only so much technical knowledge, vlogs, and presets you can look over before you either commit or don’t.

This year, it’s a goal to go on at least 10 photowalks. They can be anywhere in the city or combined with a day trip, but I have to bring my camera and I have to take photos. I’ve already got one under my belt: walking around downtown Atlanta with a new lens. But I love the mindfulness photography brings: you really observe your surroundings differently and don’t feel the need to rush anywhere but to find beauty right where I’m standing.

## 6. Removing Social Media From My Phone
This is a difficult one, not because I don’t have restraint to go on social media, but mainly because these platforms don’t make it easy to publish on other devices. I’ve started by limiting social media to my iPad for two reasons:
1. iPad social media apps aren’t great so it’s not as fun of an experience
2. My iPad Mini isn’t always with me so I’m less likely to scroll

If I do want (or rarely, need) to go on socials on my phone, I can still go through the browser but this is a pretty rough experience if you haven’t tried it. I rarely stay on there long.

## 7. Nightly Turndown
My wife and I visited an all-inclusive resort last year and we got to talking about what small things made the experience feel so luxurious. Sure there’s the all you can eat buffet and ocean breeze, but we noticed that one thing that felt calming was the hotel’s nightly turndown. Essentially, they just fold your sheets, open the balcony doors, and place a water bottle on your nightstand.

So when we got home, I started my own turndown service for the two of us. It looks like this:
1. Close our (black out) curtains
2. Fold the covers at the top (you know the way)
3. Fill up a 6oz glass of water (keeping a water jug in the bedroom for the week helps)
4. Spritz lavendar spray on the pillows
5. Turn on white noise (Calm app: rain sounds)

It sounds extra, it’s boujie. But it’s a habit that I’ve kept up for almost 4 months now and it’s a great physical practice to cue my body to start winding down.
